# Practice — Notion

## Save one page into your notebook, manually

Take one of the summaries from session 1 (Foundations, Skills, or Agents) and save it into your Notion notebook, manually. You are not writing anything of your own here, just copying ready content and arranging it into a clean page.

## What you need

1. **A Notion account**, the one you set up in the video, with your new workspace.
2. **The summary pages from session 1** (Foundations, Skills, Agents), which you already received. Take one of them, say the Foundations summary.

## The task

1. **Open a page and call it Benefits.** This is the main page that your summaries will live under.
2. **Under Benefits, open a new page** for the summary you chose (Foundations, for example).
3. **Copy and arrange.** Copy the summary content into the new page, and arrange it into blocks using the Markdown you already know: hash for a heading, dash for a list.

## Self-check

- There is a Benefits page in your notebook, and under it a page with the summary content you copied.
- The headings and lists show up as real blocks in Notion.

## Let Claude write the second page

Now hand that same work to Claude. You will copy one ready prompt along with another summary, and Claude will open a new page under Benefits and write all the content into it, straight into your Notion. You are not phrasing anything, just copying.

## What you need

1. **Your Notion account, with the Notion connector connected in Claude.** Now connect the Notion connector, exactly the way you connected your first one in Connectors. Same process, different connection.
2. **The ready prompt** (it appears below), which you copy as is.
3. **A second summary from session 1**, a different one from the one you copied in the first exercise, say the Skills summary.

## The prompt (to copy)

```
Take the following page, find the Benefits page in Notion, open a new page under it, and put all the content into it.

[paste the content of the second summary here]
```

## The task

1. **Make sure the connector is active.** Check that the Notion connector is connected inside Claude.
2. **Copy the prompt with the summary.** Copy the ready prompt, and below it paste the content of the second summary.
3. **Paste it into Claude and let it write.** Paste it all into Claude and wait for it to finish. Claude uses the connector, opens a new page under Benefits, and writes the content into it.
4. **Find the page.** Open Notion and you will see the new page under Benefits, next to the one you did manually.

## A good example

Both pages are nicely built and both are readable, and the only difference is how they got there. The first one you arranged yourself, manually; the second one Claude wrote in a second. The exact same kind of work, once by you and once by Claude. That is the whole idea.

## Why it works

- **In short:** A working connector and one more ready prompt, and that is all it takes for Claude to write straight into your notebook. The same work, just passed along.
- **In full:** You are copying a prompt someone already wrote, and adding a ready summary. The reason you did the first page manually is exactly this moment: because you did the work yourself, you can see exactly what Claude did and you can trust the result. If the page did not show up or the connector did not respond, try again, and we will sort it out in the session.
