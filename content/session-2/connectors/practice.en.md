# Connect one tool, and let Claude do the work

Connect one tool you actually work with, paste in the right prompt, and get back something real that you can use right now.

## Where you're starting from

Two things you already have:

1. **A Claude account you already work in** — you open a conversation, send a message, upload a file.
2. **One tool you genuinely use** — Gmail or Calendar. Pick the one closest to your work week.

The only requirement is an active Google account.

## The task

**1. Connect the tool.** Open a new conversation, click the plus button, and choose Gmail or Calendar. Connect through your account, and when the permissions screen appears, pause for a second and read what you're approving.

**2. Paste in the right prompt.** Copy it as is into Claude.

If you connected Gmail:

```
Go through the emails I received this week, and make me a short list: who from, about what, and what needs a reply from me. If anything is urgent, mark it at the top of the list.
```

And if you need to take time off, here's one you'll actually use:

```
Draft a formal email to my manager saying I'm going on vacation next week and asking for approval. Mention that I'm available for a handover before I leave. Save it as a draft, don't send it.
```

If you connected Calendar:

```
What's on my calendar tomorrow? Order it by time, and tell me if there are any overlaps or big gaps in the day.
```

And for planning the week:

```
Go through the coming week on my calendar, and prepare a summary: how many meetings I have each day, when the busiest day is, and where I have a free block of time for focused work.
```

**3. Use the result.** In Gmail, open the Drafts folder and you'll see the draft waiting there, ready, unsent. In Calendar, you now have a clear picture of tomorrow or of the week.

## Self-check

- The tool is connected, and the prompt returned a real result from your account.
- If you asked for a draft in Gmail, it's sitting in Drafts, and you decide whether to send it.
- You can say in one sentence what Claude saw and what it didn't: it brought back only what you asked for, it didn't load everything.

## If you got stuck

The connection moment (OAuth) is sometimes confusing: the wrong account, a window that doesn't open, or a permission that didn't go through. That's normal. Try again, and if it still won't work, we'll sort it out together at the session.
